Playing With Light

Axis Mundi
Performance / Visual Programming / 3D
A collaborative performance in which I created animated images that were projected onto dancing sails, giving life and motion to water. Axis Mundi was collectively created by Espace Art Nature over the last few years, and incorporates elements from legends and spirtuality of the native people. My goal was to create something that faithfully resonated with the group's vision.
My creative process involved 3D modeling, shader design, animation, batch editing and compositing, time-lapse photography, and was held all together by a small program I worte on Processing, which displayed the image sequences I put together, cycling continuously and fading in or out on cue.

You can take a look at this program written for Processing 3 on GitHub here.
Presented at Studios Méduse in october 2016.

Analog Modular Synth
Build / Creation
The project of building an analog synthesizer from scratch came about in 2014 (age 15) as a high school project. From bare copper boards, integrated circuits, passive components, wires and acrylic I assembled it over the course of a year, following the plans authored by Yves Usson.

Since then I've sustained a very strong interst into audio, creative means of generating sound through instrument design and most especially in the way psychoacoustics and sound perception shape our definition of music.
Here are some experiments that stemmed from this instrument. They involve a lot of feedback loops which usually don't play well with digital oscillators. You may want to lower your volume, as these fall into a world of weird, abstract and violent.
Case designed by myself using Blender and built with the help of Roger Demeule.

Audio Circuit Sketches
Design / Exploration
I have designed a timbral modifier intended to be used as a Eurorack module that exploits the complex behavior of nonlinear transformations applied to phase-distorted signals, essentially combining a classic wavefolder circuit akin to classic west-coast synthesis with a simple one pole variable-frequency phaser. (2015-2016) The result is a harmonically rich signal with strong ovetones stemming from the phase-shift frequency, deeply interacting with the input signal's harmonic content.

As time was lacking, the circuit was never actually built. However, computer models created using LTSpice and Max MSP allowed to get a faithful glimpse at it's tone; here is a simulation taking a sawtooth waveform as an input signal. Both folding amount and frequency are being modulated throughout the clip.
